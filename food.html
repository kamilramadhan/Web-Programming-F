RUJAK CINGUR

RUJAK CINGUR

Rujak cingur is a traditional food from East Java, especially in its native area of ​​Surabaya. According to historical activists in the city of Surabaya, the existence of rujak cingur in the city of Surabaya began in the 1930s, brought by immigrants from Madura Island to survive by trading in the culinary specialty, namely rujak cingur [1]. Initially traders from Madura used typical Madurese skipjack tuna petis, but to suit the taste of the people of Surabaya City, who are predominantly Javanese, they also used shrimp paste.
